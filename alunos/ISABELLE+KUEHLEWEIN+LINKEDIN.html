The Power of the Suite

Arnaud Chain

Published on LinkedIn
The fast growth of Shadow IT finds its roots in the frustration of business departments vis--vis the internal IT department. Business departments can no longer accept waiting for weeks, if not months, to get access to IT resources to develop, test and deploy applications given the level of competition in todays market. IT suppliers and IT departments need to find ways to offer business departments the same level of flexibility, agility and speed to provision technology resources, if they are going to compete with public cloud providers. At stake is their ability to influence technology decisions, and the longer it takes for IT teams to evolve, the smaller their impact will become. IT suppliers have put on the market multiple solutions; however, these have been monolithic hardware approaches incapable of reaching the expected level of flexibility. A superior solution requires IT suppliers and IT departments to overcome multiple constraints to reach this objective. Overcoming the Physical Constraint It is clearly impossible to dynamically provision either Compute, Storage or Networking capacity, when requests can only be fulfilled through available hardware capacity or through the acquisition of additional hardware. It is common that internal approval workflows, within large organisations, are slow and cumbersome. Virtualisation, introduced by VMware, for Compute, Networking and Storage overcomes this hardware dependency. Virtualisation creates an abstract layer that allows IT departments to provision virtual resources without adding new hardware capacity. IT resources can now be provisioned in a fraction of the time through Compute, Networking and Storage virtualisation. Customers are now able to demonstrate a shrinking of provisioning cycles, from weeks to minutes, or even less for simple requirements. Accelerating Integration The second constraint to overcome, in order to match the flexibility and speed of provisioning of public clouds, is integration. One approach is to rapidly provision either Computing power, or Networking bandwidth, or Storage capacity, as individual actions. More challenging is the ability to provision all three at the same time, to quickly provide a complete environment that meets business demand. What lines of business need is a comprehensive provisioning engine, that executes the requests with neither long approval workflows nor difficult synchronization between different teams, nor long delays due to an external supplier. Ensuring High Availability The engine to provision IT resources at the request of lines of business has to work without interruption. It also must be extremely reliable. High availability is the third constraint to overcome. It can only be overcome by providing an intelligent integration of the layers of compute, networking and storage virtualization, allowing each layer to evolve independently while ensuring the smooth integration of new releases.  Where Can Such An Engine Be Found? VMware created vCloud Foundation suite to meet these exact requirements. This integrated suite combines vSphere, the flagship compute virtualisation component, with NSX the networking virtualization component, and vSAN the storage virtualization component. It includes SDDC Manager to manage the lifecycle and the integration of each component, hence reducing the workload of an administrator. Moving Away From Silos The vCloud Foundation suite dramatically changes the way in which resources are provisioned within IT departments. vCloud Foundation provides the ability to overcome the typically siloed IT organization. One of the main reasons for slow provisioning comes from the difficulty to synchronize the Compute, Networking & Security and the Storage teams. With vCloud Foundation, the three components are provisioned together, allowing teams to significantly improve the way in which they offer service to their line of business customers. Because security is embedded in to every virtual machine, it becomes a more granular, and more effective component of the vCloud Foundation environment. Combining VMwares vCloud Foundation, automation and orchestration tools, IT departments are able to build a catalogue of resources that business departments can choose from, hence consuming IT resources with the same level of flexibility as public clouds. The IT department can even provide these resources from the private cloud or from a public cloud. vCloud Foundation is available on multiple public cloud offerings which allows applications to run in a private cloud, or in a public cloud, or even in hybrid mode. From Component to Solution VMware ensures its customers the highest level of choice, supporting virtual machines and containers, running in multiple environments including hundreds of public cloud offerings including  IBM Bluemix and Amazon AWS. IT departments can define the right target for each application based on objective parameters such as the cost of the resource or compliancy constraints, for example. With the vCloud Foundation suite, VMware takes up a similar challenge to Microsoft with their Office suite, or as SAP with R/3 i.e transforming a market of components into a market of solutions. Offering a logical, proven packaged solution is the only way for IT teams to offer the same level of service as public clouds, to their internal business customers. The success of vCloud Foundation in the market can give the responsibility and management of all IT resources back to the CIO, taking Shadow IT out of the shade.
